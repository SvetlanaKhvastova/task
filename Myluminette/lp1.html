What is the winter blues?

The winter blues (also called seasonal affective disorder, or SAD) is a type of depression. It happens during certain seasons of the year, most often fall or winter. It is thought that shorter days and less daylight may trigger a chemical change in the brain leading to symptoms of depression.

The demographic most affected are women
(up to 75%) 35 years and up

Are you experiencing seasonal disorder symptoms?

  • Sadness, low mood, or feelings of depression

    Loss of interest or pleasure from once enjoyable activities

  • Difficulty thinking, concentrating, or making decisions

  • Loss of energy or increased fatigue despite increased sleep hours

    Changes in appetite, eating more than usual, craving carbohydrates

Light therapy is the leading therapeutic practice for seasonal affective disorder.

FAQs

  • Is wearing the Luminette dangerous for the eyes?

    No.

    1) The light emitted by Luminette is white light enhanced with blue light. It is low intensity (1,500 lux). By way of comparison, most light therapy boxes emit light at 10,000 lux.

    2) The spectrum has been specially chosen to exclude potentially dangerous wavelengths (UV and similar).

    3) Luminette has been assessed to the standard IEC 62471 and is considered safe for the eyes.

  • How should I use Luminette glasses?

    Simply wear Luminette once a day in the morning or after lunch. Choose from three different light intensities for a session of 20, 30 or 60 minutes each day. When the session is finished, Luminette will turn off automatically.

  • How long before I begin to feel better?

    The “boosting” effect of Luminette® is almost immediate. From the very first days, you will feel your energy returning and your mood mood starting to improve. The improvements to your biological clock takes between 4 to 7 days.

  • Can I use Luminette with contact lenses or glasses?

    Yes, Luminette is compatiable with contact lenses and a pair of glasses. Simply place Luminette over your usual eyewear.

  • Can I use the Luminette if I suffer from cataracts?

    Cataracts mean the crystalline of your eye has become opaque, letting in less light. So Luminette may prove to be less effective and might need to be worn a little longer.

  • Is Luminette suitable for children?

    Because their crystallines are still growing, Luminette is not advised for children under 12 years of age.

  • Where is the Luminette manufactured?

    Apart from the case and charger, Luminette is manufactured and assembled in Belgium.

  • What is the duration of the Luminette guarantee?

    Your Luminette is guaranteed for 2 years. This guarantee covers manufacturing faults and any other fault that may occur in the context of normal device usage.

  • Are there any side effects?

    Undesirable side effects linked to Luminette are relatively slight and transitory. They may include headaches, eye fatigue or nausea. To avoid or reduce these effects, use Luminette in a well-lit room, reduce the Luminette intensity setting (there are 3 light intensity levels), do not use the device for a few days until the side effects disappear, then try again.
