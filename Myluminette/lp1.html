What is the winter blues?

The winter blues (also called seasonal affective disorder, or SAD) is a type of depression. It happens during certain seasons of the year, most often fall or winter. It is thought that shorter days and less daylight may trigger a chemical change in the brain leading to symptoms of depression.

The demographic most affected are women (up to 75%) 35 years and up

Are you experiencing seasonal disorder symptoms?

  • Sadness, low mood, or feelings of depression

    Loss of interest or pleasure from once enjoyable activities

  • Difficulty thinking, concentrating, or making decisions

  • Loss of energy or increased fatigue despite increased sleep hours

    Changes in appetite, eating more than usual, craving carbohydrates

Light therapy is the leading therapeutic practice for seasonal affective disorder.

Since 2005, light therapy has been recognized by the APA (American Psychiatric Association) as the most effective therapeutic means to treat winter blues. By rebalancing melatonin levels and the circadian rhythm, it enables the patient to regain normal sleep cycles in 2-3 weeks.
